Research

Research interests and projects.

My work sits around mathematical physics, random matrix theory, quantum chaos, machine learning, and scientific computation.

Quantum Chaos, Random Matrix Theory, and Black Holes

CERN Theory Department

This project investigates how chaotic quantum systems exhibit spectral statistics similar to random matrix ensembles. I am particularly interested in level spacings, spectral form factors, and models such as SYK in the context of holography and black hole physics.

Quantum chaos Random matrix theory SYK model Holography

Reduced Order Modeling and Machine Learning

University of South Carolina REU / continuing work

I have worked on computational approaches to reducing the cost of solving partial differential equations, including techniques based on SVD, POD, Galerkin projection, operator inference, and neural networks.

Reduced order modeling PDEs PyTorch Scientific ML

Chaotic Classical Systems

Boston University Condensed Matter Theory

Studied integrable and non-integrable systems, nonlinear perturbations, reversibility, and adiabatic limits, with applications to suppressing chaotic behavior using approximate adiabatic gauge potentials.

Classical chaos Adiabatic gauge potentials Mathematical physics
